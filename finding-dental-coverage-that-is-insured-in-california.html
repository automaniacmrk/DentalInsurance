Finding Dental Coverage that is insured In California

Health plans purchased through the Covered California Health Exchange automatically include pediatric dental benefits for children up to age 18. A lot of plans available through the Exchange include coverage for pediatric dentistry. California offers dental coverage for adults. This can be purchased as either a standalone policy or as an addition to existing health insurance. Sometimes, stand-alone plans offer richer benefits. The exception to this rule is when dental insurance is added to an employer's health plan. Sometimes, small business dental plans offer greater benefits. Orthodontia coverage can be cheaper. If your company offers group insurance, your Health Insurance Agent/Broker can help you find coverage for dental needs.

These are the terms most commonly used to describe dental insurance. Becoming acquainted with them will help you in understanding California dental insurance policies and in choosing the one that is right for you.

Uniform Dental

Uniform Dental coverage is available only if you are enrolled in Wisconsin Public Employers Group Health Insurance Program or the State of Wisconsin. This insurance covers children's, diagnostic, preventive and basic orthodontic services.

The coverage you receive for your dental needs will match the coverage you have for your other health benefits. If you choose family insurance that includes dental coverage, you'll be included in family dental coverage. Individual dental coverage will also be available if you choose individual insurance that includes dental coverage. Uniform Dental Inurance coverage is an option to add on to your insurance premium.

Enrollment continues each year unless you cancel during the annual open enrollment period.

Delta Dental Ppo

The Delta Dental PPO plan, available worldwide, provides you and your family with the flexibility to choose any licensed dentist or specialist. Your share of the cost of services depends on whether you use a dentist insurance in Delta Dental's PPO network or an out-of-network dentist.

If you choose a PPO dentist insurance plan, you will usually pay less for services. PPO dentists will accept lower fees for their services. The dentist will also complete all claims forms and send them to you free of charge. The cost of preventive dental services, including cleanings and exams, are free. Basic dentistry such as fillings or extractions is covered up to 80 percent.

Delta has more than 43,700 PPO dentists in California and 234,000 nationwide. To see a list of Delta Dental PPO dentists, visit the Delta Dental website.

Although Premier dentists at Delta Dental aren't part of the PPO network, they have agreed to pay a lower fee and will submit claims forms on your behalf. Delta Dental covers 75 percent of basic dentistry costs after annual deductible if you use a Premier dentist, up to $1,500 per year. You may need to submit a claim form to Delta Dental if you visit a non-affiliated dentist. Delta cannot assure you what percentage of the charged fees may be covered.
